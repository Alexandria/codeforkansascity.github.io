SCHEDULE

Overview

Saturday you will:

  • Get your team together
  • Define the problem and find the best solution
  • Start on the design and coding of your solution

Sunday you will:

  • Create a road map for the project
  • Present your work completed

In July teams will present their progress, to date, at an all-hands meeting. Date TBD.

In October/November final presentations will be made along with a shared celebration. Date TBD.

Join local designers, developers, doers and thinkers with a passion for making local government work better through technology.

Details

Saturday June 6th

  • 10:00 - Registration - Arrive and get checked in
  • 10:30 - Initial Talks, including Welcomes from Dean Ellen Sunni and Kansas City Chief Innovation Officer Ashley Hand
  • 10:45 - Project Pitches - 5 minutes for each team leader to pitch their project, plus open pitches
  • 11:00 Form Teams - This is the opportunity for those that are not on a team to find a team they can join.
  • 11:15 - Ice Breaker
  • 11:30 Start Work - Teams will take evaluate the skills they have, start discussing, designing and planning.
  • 12:00 - Working lunch provided
  • 12:00-8:00pm - Open hacking
  • 8:30pm - Wrap up for day

Sunday June 7th

  • 10:00am - Open hacking & dev
  • 12:00pm - Working lunch provided
  • 3:00pm - Team presentations & demos
  • 4:00pm - Awards presented for Most Innovative, Best Use of Public Data, Most Likely to Make Big Impact, and Best Story
  • 5:00pm - Wrap up & next steps

Logistics

Parking & Entrance: Parking is available in the lot to the north of the Law School or on nearby streets. For mapping purposes, the Law School's address is 500 E 52nd St Kansas City, MO 64110. Enter the building through the doors on the west side or north side. Check-in will be on the Ground Floor near the student lounge. Look for signs directing you to the Ground Floor, where all hack-tivities will be taking place.

Road Closures: The Hospital Hill Run will cause road closures the morning of the 6th. If driving, you are advised to approach the Law School from the east or the south, avoiding downtown, the Plaza and Brookside neighborhoods. Broadway and Brookside Blvd to the west/northwest of campus will be closed most of the morning. If you are approaching from the west, take 63rd Street and come up Troost or Rockhill Rd, turning east onto 55th. If you are coming from the north or east, you may approach campus from the side east of Gillham Rd (i.e. Troost or 71). A list of closures and race map can be found at hospitalhillrun.com.

If you are taking the bus, stay tuned to KCATA.org for bulletins about routes affected by the race.
