Historic Survey to Save the City...a Planning Necessity

Historic buildings provide a sense of place and continuity within our city, but did you know that a an overwhelming amount of neighborhoods and buildings have not been surveyed? Historic survey and planning go hand in hand. Within the planning process survey is a key component. It helps the planner understand the neighborhood, city and history they are working in. It helps the city identify those places that make a city great and also define those areas that may need help. Data gathered in the survey drives future policy decisions, defining what areas will be considered historic districts and potential drive development financial incentives. Survey matters.

This project will use an Architectural/Historic Inventory Form (as found on the state of Missouri’s State Historic Preservation Office’s website) to create an online application for iPad/iphone/tablet. The goal of the project is to educate on proper survey guidelines, create ease of access for preservation consultants and planners, in addition to integrating the required property data that is available from city and county resources. A broader project goal is to be able to manipulate the data in order to create maps within GIS (or customizable GoogleMaps) in order to show ownership, or property information based on survey data fields.

SafeAssist
SafeAssist is a collaborative effort initiated by the Kansas City Police Department to provide officers with information that can aid them in more effectively managing contacts with diminished capacity persons. Every year the Kansas City Police Department contacts hundreds of individuals with physical and behavioral health issues. Officers are often at a disadvantage in handling these types of service calls due to lack of information or communication impediments. SafeAssist was created to provide a vehicle for caregivers and individuals to share pertinent health and behavioral information to help both officers and individuals process the needs of those service calls much more effectively, while increasing the overall quality of service.

Partnering Blood Donation Centers, Labs, and Health Clinics
The blood supply at blood banks is often below optimal levels. Moreover, ability to create and maintain a personal health record may also be compromised among these groups. A relationship between blood donation centers, labs, safety net clinics, and hospitals could potentially improve upon both situations. Current standards dictate that when a consumer enters a blood donation center, if eligible, a donation is drawn and within 24-hours, a dozen tests are performed on the donated blood – to establish the blood type and test for infectious diseases (including HIV, Hepatitis, STDs, and local parasites and viruses). If a test result is positive, the donor is notified. It should be possible to partner with blood donation centers and labs to create a QR code associated with each donor’s unique blood specimen identifier and retrievable through a smart phone or clinic computer. The donor could be sent a link to download a QR code with their health information including that gleaned from the donation center’s self-report form and mini-physical as well as their blood type and status on the other lab-run blood tests. Any pathological findings could be red flagged and the client directed to a specialist, if needed, without requiring a visit to the safety net clinic. With permission from the consumer and a clearance code from the lab to unlock patient data (which could be automatically generated by the lab upon entrance of clinic or hospital credentials or access code), a clinic or hospital could access the QR code and easily retrieve the consumer’s information before, during, and after a visit in an effort to update and maintain the consumer’s records. The consumer would also be able to share the code when visiting a safety net clinic.

Parcel Assessment Tool
P.A.T. is designed to allow those with an interest in real estate to quickly assess if a piece of land is suitable for their project. The P.A.T.'s GIS system allows users to pinpoint locations, access the current zoning information for the space, then perform calculations to determine the specifications of an appropriate building.
