What can I work on?

We have a few projects planned, but need your ideas, too! We have already engaged Historic Kansas City, the City of KCMO, Community Capital Fund and others willing to support our projects. We just need designers, organizers, doers, and coders like you to lend a hand and a day or two to bring these ideas (and others) to life! Here are some examples of apps we can build together:

Projects

N-Hood
Build an app to help neighborhood leaders and members to use information to keep everyone aware of changes and to solve problems. For example, what businesses are opening or closing? What opportunities are there for redevelopment, what Land Bank properties are available. What upcoming construction projects/demolitions will affect the neighborhood? Are there zoning and code changes that would effect your neighborhood? Is your street going to be closed for repairs? We’d like to build on the work Code for KC has done with the CommunityKC map for neighborhoods and nonprofits to provide even more resources for those working to improve our neighborhoods and quality of life.

Team: Amanda, Leslie

Public Transit App
You are standing at a bus stop. When will the next bus come – and where could you go on it? Wouldn't it be great if you could use your smartphone to quickly access information customized to your current location.? Let's improve commuter education while promoting digital information usage.

Team: Thea

Mapping Projects
Variety of ideas for mapping tools, such as where to see STEM projects, get flu shots, where to get your taxes done, and much, much more.
Saving Historic KC
Tool to raise awareness about irreplaceable historic places we are losing forever, before the building is demolished and another parking lot is opened.

Team: Corey

STEM Project for KC STEM Alliance
Map STEM Project in KC for KC STEM Alliance
WikiKC Improvements and Additions
WikiKC.org is a living e-document pertaining to all things KC, that anyone can contribute to or edit. This will be used by anyone interested in KC or in contributing to the content. WikiKC provides an ongoing depot for posting information and telling neighborhood stories.

Project Lead: Synthia

Zoning - Parcel Assessment Tool
P.A.T. is designed to allow those with an interest in real estate to quickly assess if a piece of land is suitable for their project. The P.A.T.'s GIS system allows users to pinpoint locations, access the current zoning information for the space, then perform calculations to determine the specifications of an appropriate building.

Team: Ron, Devon, Bryan, Ryan

Single Sign On
Understand OpenID Connect. Help demonstrate the data it stores and shares, how to interacts with web applications to further advance business, law and civic use around this technology. This is similar to logging into OReilly Books with your Google account, with more functionality.

Team: Paul, Evan
